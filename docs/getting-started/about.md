# About

## Who I am

I'm **Huu Nghia**, an embedded firmware engineer specializing in Zephyr RTOS and Altium-based PCB design. I work at [SamplePCB](https://samplepcb.co.kr) in Korea.

My focus area is the physical-digital boundary — the part of a product where firmware meets silicon and silicon meets the physical world. I write drivers, design boards, debug hardware-software interactions, and document the whole process.

## What I work with

**Firmware:**
- Zephyr RTOS (primary) — devicetree, drivers, BLE stack, power management
- Bare-metal C for resource-constrained targets
- FreeRTOS (legacy projects)

**Hardware:**
- Altium Designer — schematic capture, PCB layout, design rule checks
- 4-layer stackups, controlled impedance, BGA/QFN hand soldering
- Fabrication via JLCPCB and PCBWay

**MCUs I've worked with:**
- Espressif ESP32, ESP32-S3
- STMicroelectronics STM32 series
- Espressif ESP32

## Why this site

The Zephyr RTOS documentation is comprehensive but assumes a lot. Finding a complete, honest example of a real project — one that shows the devicetree overlay, the driver code, the PCB schematic, and what actually went wrong — is rare.

This site is that resource, built from my own projects. Every page covers the full arc, not just the parts that worked cleanly.
